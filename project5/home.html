Volunteering Benefits

Volunteering at Syracuse University and in the surrounding community can provide you with numerous personal, academic, and social benefits, including:

Community Benefits

  • Strengthening Communities: Contribute to community development through collaborative events.
  • Promoting Engagement: Inspire others to get involved!
  • Addressing Local Needs: Help meet critical needs within your community.

Personal Benefits

  • Skill Development: Gain new skills or enhance existing ones like communication, problem-solving, leadership, and more!
  • Sense of Purpose: Experience giving back to causes you care about!
  • Health Benefits: Improved mental health through social connections and physical activity.

Academic Benefits

  • Enhanced Learning: Put your classroom skills to work with real-world experiences.
  • Networking Opportunities: Connect with professionals and peers, which can lead to further connections.
  • Resume Building: Strengthen your resume with valuable volunteer experiences that demonstrate commitment.

Social Benefits

  • Community Connections: Build relationships within your community and meet people from diverse backgrounds.
  • Teamwork and Collaboration: Work with others, fostering teamwork skills and friendships.
  • Cultural Awareness: Learn about different cultures and social issues.

Career Benefits

  • Experience in Your Field: Gain relevant experience that can help you enter your chosen career path.
  • References and Recommendations: Build professional relationships that can lead to positive references in the future.
