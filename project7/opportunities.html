Where to volunteer in Syracuse

Off Campus

Book Buddies Program

The Book Buddies program, a collaboration between the Syracuse City School District, United Way of Central New York, and M&T Bank, connects K-3 students with community volunteers to help them practice reading in a supportive environment. This initiative aims to strengthen students' reading skills and confidence.

To volunteer as a Book Buddy, please contact bookbuddies@unitedway-cny.org.

The Samaritan Center

The Samaritan Center is a nonprofit organization run by the Syracuse community. This nonprofit provides meals every day for those who need it, with no questions asked. The center needs weekly volunteers and offers various opportunities to choose from.

To volunteer for the Samaritan Center, please contact volunteer@samcenter.org.

Ronald McDonald Volunteer

Spend time at the Ronald McDonald House helping welcome families, taking calls, accepting donations, and more. Numerous opportunities and time frames are available.

To volunteer at the Ronald McDonald House, please contact volunteer@rmhcny.org.

In Syracuse

Best Buddies International at SU

Best Buddies International is a nonprofit dedicated to fostering one-to-one friendships, providing employment resources, and promoting leadership and inclusive living for individuals with intellectual and developmental disabilities (IDD). On campus, the focus is to raise awareness, establish friendships, and host service and activity-based events to promote our mission.

To get involved with Best Buddies International, please contact bestbuddiesatsu@gmail.com.

Food Recovery Network

Supporting the dual mission of feeding people and reducing food waste, the SU/ESF FRN recovers food from dining halls on campus to provide food for community members in need. There are 20 weekly recoveries where students can volunteer and participate.

To get involved with Food Recovery Network, please contact jkgillan@syr.edu.

Brighten A Day

A charitable organization that sends love, hope, and joy to people in need by way of cards, letters, gifts, talks, video messages, gadgets, care packages, and more. Entirely volunteer-run, this group collaborates with establishments including orphanages, hospitals, and elderly homes to spread joy.

To get involved with Brighten A Day, please contact brightenaday.syracuse@gmail.com.
