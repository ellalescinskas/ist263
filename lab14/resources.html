Resources for Getting Involved

Check out additional organizations with different ways to give back here in Syracuse:

  • Volunteer Student Spotlight: Madison Manczko ’24 created a list with descriptions of Places to Volunteer in Syracuse. Check out the article here: Volunteer Student Spotlight Article
  • Food Bank of Central New York: The food bank is a great way to get involved, offering volunteer work through food assistance initiatives. The volunteer work includes opportunities like packing food, helping at community events, and assisting with different programs. It emphasizes the impact volunteers have on reducing food insecurity in Syracuse. Whether you're looking to volunteer for a couple of hours or engage in ongoing service, the page provides all necessary information to get involved. Check out Food Bank of Central New York here: Food Bank Volunteer Page
  • Volunteer Opportunities at Local Hospitals: Local hospitals in Syracuse offer opportunities for students pursuing careers in health-related fields. These experiences are crucial for gaining knowledge, skills, and building a professional network. Check out these opportunities here: Hospital Volunteer Opportunities
  • Volunteer Central New York: Volunteer CNY serves as a platform for individuals seeking volunteer opportunities. The website offers a range of volunteer roles across different organizations, from food banks to educational programs, allowing users to find opportunities that align with their skills and interests. Check out their website here: Volunteer Central New York

My Personal Volunteer Story

Growing up, I have been involved with the nonprofit Best Buddies International, an organization close to my heart. I have taken part in their Friendship Programs since a young age, getting matched with an individual with IDD to foster a meaningful friendship. This program helps break down social barriers and promotes inclusion in our world.

Additionally, I volunteer for the Martin Richard Foundation Challenger Sports Program. In this program, you are matched with a young adult or child with developmental or physical disabilities and teach them how to play various sports. This campaign is very special to me and a great way I can get involved in my community in Boston, MA. Taking part in these volunteer opportunities has led me to create life-long friendships and connections.

Not only has it benefited me socially, but it has led me to discover new passions and strengths I never knew I had. I’ve gained valuable leadership skills by teaching sports and advocating for inclusion. Ultimately, my time volunteering has shaped my perspective and motivated me to advocate for others!
